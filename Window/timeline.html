  1. Missouri Prohibits Education of Black People

    February 16th, 1847

    On February 16, 1847, the legislature of Missouri passed an act that prohibited “Negroes and mulattoes” from learning to read and write and assembling freely for worship services. The act also forbade the migration of free Black people to the state. The 1847 law supplemented a Missouri law passed in 1825 that imposed various restrictions on free Black people. The 1825 law defined a Black person as anyone having at least one Black grandparent.

    Source: https://calendar.eji.org/racial-injustice/feb/16

  3. Civil Rights Act

    April 9th, 1866

    on April 8, 1866, the Civil Rights Act was passed. This declared all persons born in the United States and not subject to any foreign power to be citizens regardless ofany previous condition of slavery or involuntary servitude.
